How To Edit Your eBay Template

Welcome! This guide is designed to help you edit your beautiful new HTML listing seamlessly, without any coding knowledge. Just open the `.html` file in a text editor (like Notepad on PC or TextEdit on Mac) and look for the numbered blocks matching the steps below!

1

Product Title (Hero Heading)

This is the main headline for your listing. You can highlight a key text word (like a specific colourway) in purple.

<!-- ✏️ 1. PRODUCT TITLE (Hero heading) -->
<h1> Nike Air Flight 89 Cork – Black / <em>Volt</em> · UK 9 </h1>

Pro Tip: Anything you wrap inside the <em> and </em> tags will automatically be styled in the brand's vibrant purple font. Use it to highlight the most unique aspect of the shoe.

2

Tags / Badges

Under the title, you will see 4 badges describing the item. You can edit the text inside them and choose their colors.

<!-- Available classes: (none)=purple | .gold | .green -->
<span class="product-badge"><i class="..."></i> Very Rare Cork Upper</span>
<span class="product-badge gold"><i class="..."></i> Limited Release</span>

Colorizing Badges: If you leave the class as just "product-badge", it will be purple. Add the word gold or green after a space to change its theme instantly.

3

Product Images (Gallery)

This is how you swap the photos out. You will need to change the image URL (the src="...") in three places for each photo: the slide deck, the thumbnails list, and optionally the lightbox links at the very bottom.

<!-- Large Slide Image -->
<img src="./images/s-l1600 (3).webp" alt="Front view" />

<!-- Matching Thumbnail Image -->
<div class="thumb"><img src="./images/s-l1600 (3).webp" alt="Front view" /></div>

Hosting Photos: You can use standard eBay image URLs here (like https://i.ebayimg.com/...) if you upload them online first, or keep using relative folder paths if you have them saved locally!

4

Item Specifics Table & Description

This controls the quick-facts table on the right side of the page. Each row is a <tr> containing two <td> cells.

<tr>
  <td class="spec-label">Brand</td>
  <td>Nike</td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td class="spec-label">UK Size</td>
  <td><strong style="color:var(--purple)">UK 9</strong></td>
</tr>
5

Condition Rating & Meter

Change the "meter-fill" width percentage to visually show the condition. (e.g. Excellent=90%, Good=65%, Fair=40%)

<!-- Text rating -->
<span class="condition-rating">Good – Pre-owned</span>

<!-- Visual meter width -->
<div class="meter-fill" style="width:65%"></div>
6

Condition Notes List

A detailed bulletin on scuffs, sole condition, etc. Different icons show good traits vs. warnings.

<!-- Good condition item (green checkmark) -->
<li><i class="fa-solid fa-check-circle"></i><strong>Laces:</strong> Original black laces included.</li>

<!-- Warning note (yellow exclamation) -->
<li><i class="fa-solid fa-triangle-exclamation"></i><strong>Toe Area:</strong> Light scuffs visible.</li>
7

Key Features (What Makes It Special)

There are 4 feature cards at the bottom for unique selling points. You can change their icons, titles, and text.

<div class="feature-card">
  <div class="feature-icon"><i class="fa-solid fa-leaf"></i></div>
  <h4>Genuine Cork Upper</h4>
  <p>Natural cork material — every pair is completely unique.</p>
</div>

Icons: You can find thousands of icons to use for free at fontawesome.com/icons. Just search for what you want (like "box" or "shoe"), and update the class name!
